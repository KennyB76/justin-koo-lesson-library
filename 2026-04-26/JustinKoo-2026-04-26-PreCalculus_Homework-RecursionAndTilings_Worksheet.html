Problem 1 · Tower of Hanoi — Two Variants & the Shift Trick
📘 Setup — Compare the two variants
Both versions use 3 pegs A — B — C with $n$ disks (largest at the bottom). Move one disk at a time, never a larger disk onto a smaller one. The only difference is which moves are legal:
Original Hanoi
disks may move between any two pegs
$T_n = 2 T_{n-1} + 1, \;\; T_1 = 1$
Adjacent-Only Hanoi
disks move only between adjacent pegs (no A↔C)
$A_n = 3 A_{n-1} + 2, \;\; A_1 = 2$
Why the recurrences differ: In original Hanoi, the largest disk moves once (A → C directly). In adjacent-only, it must hop A → B → C (two moves), and the smaller stack has to be moved out of the way twice (A→C, then C→A) before the big disk lands at C. That gives $3 A_{n-1} + 2$ instead of $2 T_{n-1} + 1$.

Problem 2 · Characteristic Equation Method
THEOREMCharacteristic Equation for 2nd-Order Linear Homogeneous Recurrence
For the recurrence $\;a_n = p \cdot a_{n-1} + q \cdot a_{n-2}\;$ with given $a_0, a_1$:
  1. Write the characteristic equation: $\;x^2 - p\, x - q = 0\;$.
  2. Solve for roots $r_1, r_2$ (use the quadratic formula or factor).
  3. Distinct roots ($r_1 \neq r_2$): general solution is $\;a_n = C_1\, r_1^n + C_2\, r_2^n\;$.
  4. Plug in $a_0, a_1$ to get a 2×2 system → solve for $C_1, C_2$.
  5. Substitute back. The resulting closed form lets you compute $a_n$ for any $n$ without the recurrence.
Repeated root case ($r_1 = r_2 = r$): use $a_n = (C_1 + C_2 n) r^n$.
🧪 WORKED EXAMPLEDistinct integer roots — apply the 5-step method
Solve $\;a_n = 3 a_{n-1} - 2 a_{n-2}\;$ with $\;a_0 = 1, \; a_1 = 3$.
Step 1. $p = 3, \; q = -2$. Characteristic equation: $\;x^2 - 3x + 2 = 0$.
Step 2. Factor: $(x - 1)(x - 2) = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; r_1 = 1, \; r_2 = 2$.
Step 3. General solution: $\;a_n = C_1 (1)^n + C_2 (2)^n = C_1 + C_2 \cdot 2^n$.
Step 4. Plug in $a_0, a_1$:
$a_0 = 1$:  $C_1 + C_2 = 1$  …(i)
$a_1 = 3$:  $C_1 + 2 C_2 = 3$  …(ii)
Subtract (i) from (ii): $\;C_2 = 2 \;\Rightarrow\; C_1 = -1$.
Step 5. Closed form: $\;a_n = -1 + 2 \cdot 2^n = 2^{n+1} - 1$.
$\boxed{\; a_n = 2^{n+1} - 1 \;}$
