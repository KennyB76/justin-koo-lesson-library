Topic 1 — Mathematical Induction
Directions: Use the standard induction format — Base case · Inductive Hypothesis · Inductive Step · Conclusion. For #3, use strong induction.
1. Prove by induction that \(\displaystyle 1^3 + 2^3 + 3^3 + \cdots + n^3 = \left[\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2\) for all integers \(n \geq 1\).
2. Prove by induction that \(13^n - 5^n\) is divisible by \(8\) for all integers \(n \geq 1\).
3. Prove by strong induction that every integer \(n \geq 2\) can be written as a product of (one or more) prime numbers.
Topic 2 — Run-of-Heads · Recursion
Directions: For each recurrence, clearly state the base cases, the recursive relation, and the conditions on \(n\). Reduce all probabilities to lowest terms.
4. Let \(p(n)\) be the probability that in \(n\) fair coin flips there is no run of 3 heads in a row. Set up the recurrence for \(p(n)\) by splitting on how the sequence begins (T, HT, or HHT). State the values \(p(1)\), \(p(2)\), and \(p(3)\).
5. Using the recurrence from #4, compute \(p(5)\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
6. Let \(a(n)\) be the number of binary strings of length \(n\) with no two consecutive 1's. Write the recurrence (with base cases) and compute \(a(6)\).
Topic 3 — Series · Σ · Domain of Convergence
Directions: Use the appropriate arithmetic / finite-geometric / infinite-geometric formula. For #8, state the inequality on \(x\) that guarantees \(|r| < 1\) and give the closed-form sum on that domain.
7. Find the sum \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{20} (3k - 2)\). (Arithmetic.)
8. Determine the domain of convergence (the set of \(x\) for which the series converges) and give the sum in closed form for \[ 5 \;+\; 5\!\left(\dfrac{x+1}{2}\right) \;+\; 5\!\left(\dfrac{x+1}{2}\right)^{\!2} \;+\; 5\!\left(\dfrac{x+1}{2}\right)^{\!3} \;+\; \cdots \]
9. A geometric series has first term \(a = 6\) and common ratio \(r = \dfrac{2}{3}\). Find \(S_\infty\), and find the smallest \(n\) such that \(S_n\) is within \(0.01\) of \(S_\infty\).
Topic 4 — Bernoulli's Madness
Directions: Use sums of powers, the hockey stick identity, telescoping, and the closed-form trick for the linear recurrence \(T_n = a\,T_{n-1} + b\). Express each answer in closed form, then evaluate.
10. Use the hockey stick identity to evaluate \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{N} k(k+1)(k+2)\) in closed form.
11. Use telescoping to evaluate \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{N} \dfrac{1}{k(k+1)}\).
12. Solve the linear recurrence \(T_n = 2\,T_{n-1} + 3\) with \(T_0 = 1\). Find a closed form for \(T_n\) and compute \(T_5\).
